# Unit Economics

## Value of a Converted Lead
If a startup is overspending by $1,000/month, and Credex sells discounted credits at a 20% margin, capturing that $1k/mo spend represents $200/month ($2,400/year) in gross profit for Credex. Assuming an average customer lifespan of 2 years, the Lifetime Value (LTV) of a converted mid-market lead is roughly **$4,800**.

## Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
Using the GTM plan:
- **Cold Outbound / Communities (Organic):** Only cost is time. Assuming an intern/SDR spends 10 hours to get 50 visitors, and 1 converts, at $30/hr, the CAC is **$300**.
- **Social Media Tear-downs:** High leverage. One viral post might take 2 hours ($60) and generate 500 visitors, leading to 3 conversions. CAC is **$20**.
- **Overall Blended CAC Target:** For a free utility tool acting as a lead magnet, we should target a blended CAC of **<$100** per booked consultation.

## Profitability Funnel Thresholds
For this tool to be profitable, the following funnel conversion rates need to hold true:
1. **Visitor to Audit Completed:** 20%
2. **Audit Completed to Email Captured:** 15% (for those seeing >$100 savings)
3. **Email Captured to Consultation Booked:** 10%
4. **Consultation to Credit Purchase (Close Rate):** 25%

*Math Check:* 10,000 visitors → 2,000 audits → 300 emails → 30 consultations → 7.5 closed deals.
7.5 deals * $4,800 LTV = $36,000 in LTV generated from 10,000 visitors.

## Driving $1M ARR in 18 Months
To generate $1,000,000 in ARR, assuming an Average Contract Value (ACV) of $12,000/year ($1k/mo spend moving to Credex), we need **84 active customers**.
At a 25% close rate from consultations, we need **336 consultations**.
At a 10% booking rate from emails, we need **3,360 captured leads**.
At a 15% capture rate, we need **22,400 completed audits** showing high savings.

**What must be true:**
1. The tool must have a strong viral loop (shareable URLs) so that CAC remains near zero as volume scales to 22k+ audits.
2. The AI tool ecosystem must remain fragmented enough that startups continue to double-pay for overlapping tools.
